- **400 grants, and counting.** We crossed **400 grants awarded** this
  quarter. The first one went out in July 2023.
- **10,904 pull requests.** Our grantees cited that many in their 2025
  progress reports. The fuller picture is in our
  [2025 Year in Review](/blog/2025-year-in-review), out late January.
- **Machine-checked Bitcoin cryptography.** We funded
  [remix7531](/blog/sixteenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants#advancing-formal-verification-for-bitcoin-cryptography)
  in February to build formal proofs in [F*](https://fstar-lang.org/) (via
  the [HAX toolchain](https://cryspen.com/hax-toolchain/)) for
  [RustCrypto](https://github.com/RustCrypto/)'s `sha2` and `k256` crates and
  for the multi-scalar multiplication routine used in batch signature
  verification on the `secp256k1` curve. Remix7531 joined the
  [Bitcoin Optech #401 recap podcast](https://bitcoinops.org/en/podcast/2026/04/21/)
  on April 21 to talk about the work.
- **An impact report on nostr relays.** Our March report
  [Advancements in Nostr Relays](/blog/advancements-in-nostr-relays) covers
  ten projects across relay software, hosting, discovery, personal relays,
  and content filtering. Most of this work is invisible to end users, and
  keeps the network resilient as it grows.

## Software Shipped

1. **Splicing ratified in the Lightning spec.** After years of work by
   [Dusty Daemon](https://github.com/dunxen) and collaborators, splicing is
   now a first-class part of the [Lightning BOLTs](https://github.com/lightning/bolts).
   Channel capacity can be added or removed without closing the channel
   on-chain.
2. **Private broadcast merged into Bitcoin Core.**
   [PR #29415](https://github.com/bitcoin/bitcoin/pull/29415) merged in late
   January after 2 years and 9 months of review. Nodes can now broadcast
   their own transactions over Tor or I2P while still peering on clearnet
   for blocks.
3. **[Bitcoin Research Kit](https://github.com/bitcoinresearchkit/brk/releases)
   hit stable.** BRK went from pre-release to `v0.2.0` this quarter through
   a full architectural rewrite, shipping 19 BRK releases and 25 releases of
   the underlying [anydb](https://github.com/anydb-rs/anydb) database.
4. **Stratum V2: [SRI v1.7.0](https://github.com/stratum-mining/stratum/releases)
   and [sv2-apps v0.1.0](https://github.com/stratum-mining/sv2-apps/releases).**
   The reference implementation shipped its 1.7.0 release, and the
   newly-split `sv2-apps` repository got its first tagged release.
5. **Payjoin 1.0 release candidate.**
   [payjoin-1.0.0-rc.2](https://github.com/payjoin/rust-payjoin/releases/tag/payjoin-1.0.0-rc.2)
   shipped in late February, along with new
   [C# bindings](https://github.com/payjoin/rust-payjoin/pull/1318),
   [uniffi-dart v0.1.0](https://github.com/Uniffi-Dart/uniffi-dart/releases),
   and payjoin support landing in
   [bdk-cli v3.0.0](https://github.com/bitcoindevkit/bdk-cli/releases/tag/v3.0.0).
6. **Fedimint [v0.10.0 "Lighthouse"](https://github.com/fedimint/fedimint/releases/tag/v0.10.0).**
   The Lightning Gateway moved from CLI-only to a full UI, and the release
   shipped as a mainnet [Start9](https://start9.com/) package.
7. **[Amethyst v1.04 and v1.05](https://github.com/vitorpamplona/amethyst/releases).**
   Vitor Pamplona shipped two major Amethyst releases (752 commits this
   quarter) and merged NIP-85 Trusted Assertions and NIP-A4 Public Messages
   for decentralized web-of-trust and simpler public DMs.
8. **[Damus v0.7.0 and v0.7.1](https://github.com/damus-io/damus/releases).**
   jb55 shipped two Damus releases, upgraded nostrdb to 0.9.0, and launched
   Damus Labs behind Purple for early-access features.
9. **Frostr [Igloo iOS](https://github.com/FROSTR-ORG) shipped on the App
   Store, plus [Bifrost v2](https://github.com/FROSTR-ORG) in Rust/WASM.**
   FROST-based nostr multisig is now available across all major platforms.
10. **[nostr-relay TypeScript framework](https://github.com/CodyTseng/nostr-relay).**
    Cody extracted the core of
    [nostr-relay-nestjs](https://github.com/CodyTseng/nostr-relay-nestjs)
    into a standalone library. Other relays, including one-click personal
    relays, now build on it.

More on the relay side of things in our
[March impact report on nostr relays](/blog/advancements-in-nostr-relays).
