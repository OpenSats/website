<div className="editors-note">

I could write endless pages on the projects that OpenSats has funded, and
maybe one day I will. But today, let me do the opposite instead: talk about
what OpenSats _doesn't_ fund, and why.

We don't fund anything that is closed source. FOSS is non-negotiable.
"Source viewable" doesn't cut it either. All projects must have a proper
license that makes the source code
[free as in freedom](/faq/application#what-does-it-mean-to-produce-work-under-a-free-and-open-source-license).

We don't fund anything that has a shitcoin attached to it. We are bitcoin
only, and always will be. Money printing takes from the many and gives to
the few, which is clearly and obviously immoral. It's a travesty that money
printing is so widespread and normalized as it is today. And while fiat
money is the worst offender, shitcoins are a close second. We exist to
solve the problem of sustainable funding, and projects that print their
own money don't have a funding problem, obviously. (Money printing isn't
sustainable long-term either, but that's a story for another day.)

We don't fund for-profit companies, or anything that could be monetized
easily. We are not a VC, or an incubator, or an entity that invests in
projects, products, or startups. Investments and profits are important,
but OpenSats is focusing on something else: providing a sustainable and
consistent ecosystem to fund FOSS contributors. That was
[our mission statement from Day 1](/mission), and it will continue to be
going forward.

In the next couple of years we hope to move closer and closer towards our
goal of long-term sustainability. The plan is simple: make more bitcoiners,
encourage bitcoiners to give back to open-source projects directly, help
said projects to become self-sustaining, and encourage other orgs to do
the same.
["Let a thousand flowers bloom"](/blog/let-a-thousand-flowers-bloom) and
all that.

My hope is that OpenSats will eventually become obsolete. Until it is, we
are dependent on donors who believe, like we do, that free and open-source
software is paramount to a free and prosperous society. No freedom without
FOSS, no prosperity without bitcoin.

<p className="editors-note__signoff">— Gigi</p>

</div>

As mentioned in our latest
[end-of-year report](/blog/2025-year-in-review), the dream is to have
OpenSats supported by the public, i.e. the millions of users who benefit
from advancements made in the open-source software we support. We are a
long stretch away from that goal. **Less than 1%** of users donate to the FOSS
ecosystem. If you get value from using the freedom tech we fund, please
consider making a donation, either directly to the projects, or via one of
our funds.

- **400 grants, and counting.** We crossed **400 grants awarded** this
  quarter. The first one went out in July 2023.
- **10,904 pull requests.** Our grantees cited that many in their 2025
  progress reports. The fuller picture is in our
  [2025 Year in Review](/blog/2025-year-in-review), out late January.
- **Machine-checked Bitcoin cryptography.** We funded
  [remix7531](/blog/sixteenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants#advancing-formal-verification-for-bitcoin-cryptography)
  in February to build formal proofs in [F*](https://fstar-lang.org/) (via
  the [HAX toolchain](https://cryspen.com/hax-toolchain/)) for
  [RustCrypto](https://github.com/RustCrypto/)'s `sha2` and `k256` crates and
  for the multi-scalar multiplication routine used in batch signature
  verification on the `secp256k1` curve. Remix7531 joined the
  [Bitcoin Optech #401 recap podcast](https://bitcoinops.org/en/podcast/2026/04/21/)
  on April 21 to talk about the work.
- **An impact report on nostr relays.** Our March report
  [Advancements in Nostr Relays](/blog/advancements-in-nostr-relays) covers
  ten projects across relay software, hosting, discovery, personal relays,
  and content filtering. Most of this work is invisible to end users, and
  keeps the network resilient as it grows.

- **[Payjoin](/projects/payjoin) release candidate:**
  [payjoin-1.0.0-rc.2](https://github.com/payjoin/rust-payjoin/releases/tag/payjoin-1.0.0-rc.2)
  shipped in late February, along with new
  [C# bindings](https://github.com/payjoin/rust-payjoin/pull/1318),
  [uniffi-dart v0.1.0](https://github.com/Uniffi-Dart/uniffi-dart/releases),
  and payjoin support landing in
  [bdk-cli v3.0.0](https://github.com/bitcoindevkit/bdk-cli/releases/tag/v3.0.0).
- **[Stratum V2](/projects/stratumv2):
  [SRI v1.8.0](https://github.com/stratum-mining/stratum/releases/tag/v1.8.0)
  and
  [sv2-apps v0.1.0](https://github.com/stratum-mining/sv2-apps/releases).**
  The reference implementation shipped two releases this quarter
  ([v1.7.0](https://github.com/stratum-mining/stratum/releases/tag/v1.7.0)
  in January and
  [v1.8.0](https://github.com/stratum-mining/stratum/releases/tag/v1.8.0)
  in March), and the newly-split `sv2-apps` repo got its first tagged
  release.
- **[Splicing](/projects/splicing) lands in the Lightning spec:** After
  years of work by
  [Dusty Daemon](/blog/dusty-daemon-receives-lts-grant) and collaborators,
  splicing is now a [BOLT](https://github.com/lightning/bolts). Channel
  capacity can be added or removed without closing the channel on-chain.
- **Private broadcast, merged into Bitcoin Core:**
  [PR #29415](https://github.com/bitcoin/bitcoin/pull/29415) landed in late
  January after lots of discussions and in-depth review. Nodes can now
  broadcast their own transactions over Tor or I2P while still peering on
  clearnet for blocks. Our LTS grantee
  [Vasil Dimov](/blog/vasil-dimov-receives-lts-grant) led the effort.

[Operations Budget](/funds/ops) is kept strictly separate. If you'd like to
help sustain this work, please consider setting up a recurring donation.
