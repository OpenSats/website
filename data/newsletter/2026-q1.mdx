## What happened in Q1, 2026?

Between January and March 2026, OpenSats announced four rounds of grants —
[seventeen Bitcoin projects](/blog/sixteenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants), [three
nostr projects](/blog/fifteenth-wave-of-nostr-grants), [five Bitcoin Core
contributors](/blog/five-grants-to-strengthen-bitcoin-development), and [nine
more Bitcoin projects](/blog/seventeenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants) — alongside
our impact report on [nostr relays](/blog/advancements-in-nostr-relays), our
[2025 Year in Review](/blog/2025-year-in-review), and new funding from the
[Human Rights Foundation](/blog/opensats-receives-funding-from-the-human-rights-foundation)
and the [Bitwise Bitcoin ETF](/blog/opensats-receives-donation-from-bitwise-etf).

## New grants, Q1 2026

### Bitcoin & Lightning

The [sixteenth](/blog/sixteenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants) and
[seventeenth](/blog/seventeenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants) waves and a dedicated
round of [Bitcoin Core grants](/blog/five-grants-to-strengthen-bitcoin-development)
brought funding to Core contributors, wallets, libraries, mining, ecash, and
hardware signing.

- **[Specter DIY](/blog/seventeenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants#specter-diy)** — open
  hardware signing device users can assemble and audit themselves, with a
  MicroPython firmware that [SeedSigner](/projects/seedsigner), Krux, and others
  build on.
- **[JoinMarket NG](/blog/seventeenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants#joinmarket-ng)** —
  a reimplementation of JoinMarket that modernizes a peer-to-peer CoinJoin
  protocol and adds nostr-based offer broadcasting for censorship resistance.
- **[Noah](/blog/sixteenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants#noah)** — a mobile self-custody
  wallet for the Ark protocol from the creator of Blixt, collapsing Lightning
  and on-chain into a single balance.
- **[Rust-Bitcoin & rust-secp256k1](/blog/sixteenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants#rust-bitcoin)** —
  continuing support for the foundational Rust crates that BDK, LDK, electrs,
  and Fedimint depend on.
- **[Stratum V2](/projects/stratumv2)** — further work on the reference
  implementation, with improved testing, tooling, and documentation for
  encrypted, decentralized pooled mining.
- **[Five Bitcoin Core contributors](/blog/five-grants-to-strengthen-bitcoin-development)** —
  janb84 on release testing and PR review, naiyoma on P2P privacy and node
  fingerprinting research, purpleKarrot on build-system and C++ modernization,
  plus returning grantees danielabrozzoni and kevkevinpal.

See the [full list of Bitcoin grantees](/tags/bitcoin) for everyone funded this
quarter.

### Nostr

The [fifteenth wave of nostr grants](/blog/fifteenth-wave-of-nostr-grants)
funded three projects exploring what else a permissionless identity and
transport layer can be used for.

- **[ContextVM](/blog/fifteenth-wave-of-nostr-grants#contextvm)** — an
  [MCP](https://modelcontextprotocol.io/)-over-nostr protocol that lets AI tools
  and services be addressed by public key, without domains, OAuth, or port
  forwarding.
- **[Nostube](/blog/fifteenth-wave-of-nostr-grants#nostube)** — a video platform
  on nostr with uploads, playlists, zaps, and distributed Cashu-paid
  transcoding.
- **[TollGate](/blog/fifteenth-wave-of-nostr-grants#tollgate)** — firmware that
  turns OpenWrt-compatible routers into pay-as-you-go WiFi access points, with
  users paying in ecash and routers discovering each other over nostr.

## Impact report: nostr relays

Relays are the substrate nostr runs on: simple, permissionless servers that
accept, store, and forward events. Our March report, [Advancements in Nostr
Relays](/blog/advancements-in-nostr-relays), looks at ten projects we've funded
across relay software, hosting, discovery, personal relays, and content
filtering — the largely invisible plumbing that keeps the network censorship-
resistant as it grows.

## Funding news

- **[Human Rights Foundation](/blog/opensats-receives-funding-from-the-human-rights-foundation)** —
  a $116,000 grant to our Operations Budget as part of HRF's Q4 2025 Bitcoin
  Development Fund round.
- **[Bitwise Bitcoin ETF](/blog/opensats-receives-donation-from-bitwise-etf)** —
  a recurring donation to our General Fund, part of [BITB](https://bitbetf.com/)'s
  pledge of 10% of annual gross profits to Bitcoin open-source development.

For a fuller picture, our [2025 Year in Review](/blog/2025-year-in-review) is
the best place to start.
