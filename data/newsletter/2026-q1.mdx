- **400 grants, and counting.** We crossed **400 grants awarded** this
  quarter. The first one went out in July 2023.
- **Machine-checked Bitcoin cryptography.** We funded
  [remix7531](/blog/sixteenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants#advancing-formal-verification-for-bitcoin-cryptography)
  in February to build formal proofs in [F*](https://fstar-lang.org/) (via
  the [HAX toolchain](https://cryspen.com/hax-toolchain/)) for
  [RustCrypto](https://github.com/RustCrypto/)'s `sha2` and `k256` crates and
  for the multi-scalar multiplication routine used in batch signature
  verification on the `secp256k1` curve.
- **An impact report on nostr relays.** Our March report
  [Advancements in Nostr Relays](/blog/advancements-in-nostr-relays) covers
  ten projects across relay software, hosting, discovery, personal relays,
  and content filtering. Most of this work is invisible to end users, and
  keeps the network resilient as it grows.

## Sats Received

- **A $116,000 operating grant from the Human Rights Foundation.** HRF's
  Q4 2025 Bitcoin Development Fund round included us in the
  [January announcement](/blog/opensats-receives-funding-from-the-human-rights-foundation).
  HRF funding is earmarked for our Operations Budget, kept strictly separate
  from the 100%-pass-through grant funds.
- **Promise made, promise kept.**
  [Bitwise's BITB ETF](/blog/opensats-receives-donation-from-bitwise-etf)
  began sending a share of its gross profits to our General Fund this
  quarter. It's part of the 10% pledge Bitwise made at launch; Brink and
  HRF's Bitcoin Development Fund get the other cuts.
- **If you missed 2025.** Our [2025 Year in Review](/blog/2025-year-in-review)
  went out in late January. Headline numbers: **674 donations totaling
  $3,158,680**, a **$2M gift from the Reynolds Foundation** (the single
  largest of the year), **2,821 grant-application evaluations** by the
  board and committee, and **10,904 pull requests** referenced in grantee
  progress reports.

## Software Shipped

{/* --------------------------------------------------------------------- */}
{/* SOFTWARE SHIPPED. Concrete grantee deliverables from Q1 2026. Keep    */}
{/* it to things that actually shipped code this quarter, not grant       */}
{/* announcements.                                                        */}

- **[nostr-relay framework](https://github.com/CodyTseng/nostr-relay).**
  Cody extracted the core of
  [nostr-relay-nestjs](https://github.com/CodyTseng/nostr-relay-nestjs) into
  a standalone TypeScript library. Other relays, including one-click
  personal relays, now build on it.
- **[Nostr Feedz](https://github.com/PlebOne/Nostr-Feedz) got two-way sync.**
  PlebOne shipped two-way sync and category support in January, along with
  a Chrome extension and a command-line client. Nostr Feedz bridges
  long-form nostr content and RSS readers.
- **[PlebOne Relay](https://github.com/PlebOne/relay.pleb.one) redesigned
  its login.** NIP-05 verification went live in January, so users of the
  free, web-of-trust relay can claim a human-readable nostr address at
  no cost.

More in our [March impact report on nostr relays](/blog/advancements-in-nostr-relays).

## Support Open-Source

100% of donations to our funds go directly to open-source projects; our
[Operations Budget](/funds/ops) is kept strictly separate. If you'd like to
help sustain this work, please consider setting up a recurring donation.
