---
title: 'Silent Payments'
summary: 'A way to receive Bitcoin to a reusable static address without reusing on-chain addresses.'
category: 'Bitcoin'
aliases: ['BIP 352']
---

Silent Payments, specified in [BIP 352](https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0352.mediawiki), let a receiver publish a single static address that a sender can pay many times. Each payment lands on a different, unlinkable on-chain output. There is no interaction between sender and receiver beyond knowing the static address.

The trade-off is on the receiver side: a wallet has to scan every block for outputs that could belong to it, which is more work than checking a fixed script. Light clients and specialized indexers are active areas of work.

Silent Payments remove the old choice between address reuse and address rotation schemes such as BIP 47. A donation address or a shop address can stay the same in public without leaking a payment history.
