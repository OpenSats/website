The Satoshi Nakamoto Institute advances and preserves knowledge of Bitcoin's history, economics, and technology. It hosts an archive of Satoshi's public writings, a large library of essays and source material, curated collections, podcasts, and original publishing that help people study Bitcoin in its broader historical and intellectual context.

SNI matters because Bitcoin needs durable historical memory, accessible archives, and serious educational work that keeps foundational ideas available to anyone who wants to learn. The Institute has become one of the key reference points for that work.

## Why fund it?

Bitcoin education compounds. A well-maintained archive, library, and publishing effort can help students, writers, researchers, builders, and curious newcomers for years. Open access matters here. The more durable and searchable these materials become, the more useful they are to the wider ecosystem.

OpenSats supported SNI through the [OpenSats Education Initiative][education]. That support helps preserve important source material and expand the educational surface area around Bitcoin.

## What's next?

Recent work has focused on designing Archive v2, a more durable archival system built around established preservation standards and better ingest workflows. The goal is to turn SNI from a website that hosts historical material into a proper digital archive that can scale to mailing lists, forums, IRC logs, and other primary-source collections.

Alongside that archival work, SNI continues to publish new material, expand translations, improve the website, and maintain one of the strongest open libraries in Bitcoin.
