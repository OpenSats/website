The Satoshi Nakamoto Institute preserves and publishes foundational Bitcoin material. It hosts an archive of Satoshi's public writings, a large library of essays and source material, curated collections, podcasts, and original writing that help readers study Bitcoin's history, economics, and technical roots.

SNI matters because Bitcoin needs archives as much as it needs code. People need primary sources, historical context, and durable public references they can return to years later. SNI has become one of the main places people go for that work.

## Why fund it?

A well-maintained library and publishing effort can help students, writers, researchers, builders, and curious newcomers for years. Open access matters here. The more searchable and durable these materials become, the more useful they are to the wider ecosystem.

OpenSats supported SNI through the [OpenSats Education Initiative][education]. That support helps preserve important source material and fund the ongoing work of publishing, maintenance, and archival improvement.

## What's next?

Recent work has focused on Archive v2, a more durable archival system built around established preservation standards and better ingest workflows. The goal is to make SNI a real digital archive that can handle mailing lists, forums, IRC logs, and other primary-source collections at scale.

Alongside that archival work, SNI continues to publish new material, expand translations, improve the website, and maintain a public library of essential Bitcoin texts.
