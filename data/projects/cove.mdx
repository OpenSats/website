Cove is a single-signature Bitcoin wallet for iOS and Android, built on
[BDK](/projects/bdk). The app logic is written in Rust with native UI in SwiftUI
and Jetpack Compose. It is already available on the iOS App Store, while the
Android application is in development.

The wallet is designed around hardware signing devices. It supports
[TAPSIGNER](https://tapsigner.com/) and
[SATSCARD](https://store.coinkite.com/store/satscard) via NFC, and
moves [PSBTs](https://bitcoinops.org/en/topics/psbt/) between phone
and signing device using QR codes (including animated
[BBQr](https://github.com/bitcoinppl/bbqr-ffi) sequences) or virtual drives.
Air-gapped workflows keep private keys off the phone entirely.

Other features include [BIP-329](https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0329.mediawiki)
labels for organizing transactions, detailed coin control, custom fee settings, watch-only mode,
and a decoy PIN that opens a limited version of the wallet under duress. Users can
connect to their own Electrum or Esplora node.

## Why fund it?

Good wallet UX for hardware signers is hard to get right on mobile. Most Bitcoin
wallets focus on Lightning or multisig; Cove fills a gap for people who want a
simple on-chain wallet that works well with Coldcard, Krux, or TAPSIGNER. Praveen
has also contributed upstream improvements to
[`rust-cktap`](https://github.com/notmandatory/rust-cktap),
[`uniffi`](https://github.com/mozilla/uniffi-rs), and BIP-329 libraries that
benefit the broader Rust Bitcoin ecosystem.

OpenSats first funded Cove in the [fifth wave of Bitcoin
grants](/blog/bitcoin-grants-july-2024#cove) in July 2024 and has renewed the
grant [multiple times](/blog/sixteenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants) since.

## What's next?

Current priorities include the Android release, SATSCARD spending support, UTXO
lock/unlock, and continued UI refinements. For a detailed look at recent
progress, see the [Advancements in Bitcoin and Lightning
Wallets](/blog/advancements-in-bitcoin-and-lightning-wallets#cove) impact report.
