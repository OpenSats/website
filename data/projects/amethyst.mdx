Amethyst is a nostr client for Android built by [Vitor
Pamplona](/blog/vitor-pamplona-receives-lts-grant). It covers the basics you'd
expect from a social app (feeds, profiles, DMs, group chats) but also supports
live streams, marketplaces, long-form articles, voice notes, zaps via Wallet
Connect, media feeds, and more. It ships with built-in Tor
support enabled by default, so relay operators never see your IP address.

Amethyst recently moved to the
[outbox model](https://mikedilger.com/gossip-model/), joining other clients that
have adopted this approach to relay management. Instead of users manually
picking which relays to read from, the app figures that out automatically based
on who you follow. On mobile, Amethyst can connect to over 1,000 relays at once
without noticeable battery or bandwidth cost. It also gives users granular
control over every relay type: inbox, outbox, DM, search, indexer, broadcast,
local, and more.

> This achievement is a major demonstration of the true decentralized potential
> of nostr. By enabling mobile clients to automatically connect to 1,000 relays,
> we have fundamentally shifted the narrative. We've proven that users can be
> reliably served by a highly distributed network, not just a few central
> points.
>
> <cite>—[Vitor Pamplona](/blog/advancements-in-nostr-clients#amethyst)</cite>

Amethyst was one of the first clients to implement
[NIP-17](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/17.md) for
encrypted DMs, and it supports encrypted media uploads in private conversations.
Vitor also helped design and ship
[NIP-85](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/85.md), which
brings Web of Trust scoring to nostr, letting users choose trust providers to
filter spam and surface reputable accounts.

## Why fund it?

Amethyst is one of the most widely used nostr clients on Android and has been a
testing ground for many new NIPs. Vitor doesn't just build a client; he writes
protocol proposals, builds reference implementations, and pushes the ecosystem
forward. His work on the outbox model, encrypted DMs, and Web of Trust has
influenced how other clients approach these problems too.

Beyond Amethyst itself, the project has produced
[Quartz](https://github.com/vitorpamplona/amethyst/tree/main/quartz), a Kotlin
library for nostr that other Android and Kotlin Multiplatform projects can use.

OpenSats first funded Amethyst in the [first wave of nostr
grants](/blog/nostr-grants-july-2023#amethyst) in July 2023. Vitor later
received a [long-term support grant](/blog/vitor-pamplona-receives-lts-grant) in
August 2024, which lets him work on Amethyst full-time.

## What's next?

Recent work has focused on Web of Trust integration (NIP-85), a high-performance
local database with sub-microsecond queries for Quartz, follow lists and starter
packs, and a redesigned voice notes UI. The Quartz library is being migrated to
Kotlin Multiplatform, which will open the door to Amethyst Desktop and Amethyst
iOS ports that are now in development by other contributors.

The next quarter will focus on WoT-based content filtering to replace legacy
spam filters, and continued support for the desktop and iOS efforts. For a
detailed look at recent progress, see the [Advancements in Nostr
Clients](/blog/advancements-in-nostr-clients#amethyst) impact report.
