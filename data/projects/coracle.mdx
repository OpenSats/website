Coracle is a nostr web client built by
[Hodlbod](/blog/hodlbod-receives-lts-grant). It started as a social feed reader
but has grown to cover group chat, direct messages, communities, marketplaces,
and classifieds. The client runs in the browser and works across desktop and
mobile.

Where Coracle puts the most effort is relay handling. Users can choose which
relays receive a given note, and the outbox model figures out where to fetch
posts from based on who you follow. Smarter fallback behavior keeps timelines
loading even when individual relays go down.
[Negentropy](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/77.md)-based
sync makes refreshing feeds faster by only pulling what changed. A restored
global feed gives newcomers an easy way to browse the wider network.

Hodlbod also works on relay infrastructure directly. He proposed and implemented
[NIP-43](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/43.md) for relay
membership lists and built [zooid](https://github.com/coracle-social/zooid), a
relay that uses them. Combined with
[NIP-86](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/86.md) for relay
admin controls, these features let relays state who they serve and what content
they host.

## Why fund it?

Coracle is one of the longer-running nostr clients and has been a testing ground
for relay-related protocol improvements. Hodlbod ships NIP implementations in
Coracle, tests them in production, and feeds results back into the spec process.
[Daniele](/blog/daniele-receives-lts-grant) contributes UX and reliability work.

The project has also produced
[Welshman](https://github.com/coracle-social/welshman), a library extracted from
Coracle that other nostr projects can use, and
[Triflector](https://github.com/coracle-social/triflector), a relay
implementation.

OpenSats first funded Coracle in the [first wave of nostr
grants](/blog/nostr-grants-july-2023#coracle) in July 2023. Hodlbod later
received a [long-term support grant](/blog/hodlbod-receives-lts-grant).

## What's next?

Recent work has focused on web-of-trust feed filtering, proof-of-work display
and generation, and per-note relay selection in the compose flow. For a
detailed look at recent progress, see the [Advancements in Nostr
Clients](/blog/advancements-in-nostr-clients#coracle) impact report.
