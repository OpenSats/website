0xchat is a chat-first nostr client for iOS and Android built by
[water783](https://njump.me/npub10td4yrp6cl9kmjp9x5yd7r8pm96a5j07lk5mtj2kw39qf8frpt8qm9x2wl).
Users get one-to-one encrypted DMs, private and public groups, channels, voice
notes, and audio and video calls. Relay-based signaling lets callers connect
without a central server; the audio and video streams go through user-selected
ICE servers.

The app implements multiple DM formats: the older NIP-04 encrypted messages, the
sealed-sender and gift-wrapped format from
[NIP-17](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/17.md) and
[NIP-59](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/59.md), and a
secret chat mode. That interoperability lets 0xchat users carry DM threads
with people on Amethyst, Damus, Coop, Flotilla, and noStrudel without leaving
their preferred app.

0xchat also supports Lightning zaps, Nostr Wallet Connect for balance and
payment flows, and [NIP-46](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/46.md)
remote signing via Amber, nsec.app, and other signers.

## Why fund it?

Encrypted messaging is one of the few use cases where nostr has a chance to
replace an existing centralized product for everyday users. A usable mobile
client with group chats, calls, and an onboarding flow that people already
recognize from other messengers pushes that story forward. 0xchat is also one
of the earliest clients to ship new DM standards, which helps the rest of the
ecosystem converge on interoperable private messaging.

OpenSats first funded 0xchat in the
[second wave of nostr grants](/blog/nostr-grants-october-2023#0xchat) in
October 2023 and has renewed the grant multiple times, most recently in the
[thirteenth wave](/blog/thirteenth-wave-of-nostr-grants).

## What's next?

Recent releases continue to polish the UI and expand group and channel
features, with ongoing work on giftwrap DMs, media handling, and call
reliability. The team also ships regularly to the iOS App Store, Google Play,
[Zapstore](https://zapstore.dev/apps/com.oxchat.nostr), and F-Droid.
