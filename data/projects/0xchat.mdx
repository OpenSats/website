0xchat is a chat-first nostr client built by
[water783](https://njump.me/npub10td4yrp6cl9kmjp9x5yd7r8pm96a5j07lk5mtj2kw39qf8frpt8qm9x2wl)
and a small team of contributors. It runs on iOS, Android, and the desktop
(macOS, Windows, Linux), with a tablet-optimized layout. Users get one-to-one
encrypted DMs, private and public groups, channels, voice notes, and audio and
video calls backed by a full CallKit implementation on iOS and
ConnectionService on Android. Relay-based signaling lets callers connect
without a central server; the audio and video streams go through user-selected
ICE servers.

The app implements multiple DM formats: the older NIP-04 encrypted messages,
the sealed-sender and gift-wrapped format from
[NIP-17](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/17.md) and
[NIP-59](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/59.md), a secret
chat mode, and [NIP-104](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/pull/1427)
Nostr MLS (Messaging Layer Security) for forward-secure group chats. 0xchat
was also the first Nostr client to ship
[NIP-29](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/29.md)
relay-managed groups. This interoperability lets 0xchat users carry DM threads
with people on Amethyst, Damus, Coop, Flotilla, and noStrudel without leaving
their preferred app.

0xchat also supports Lightning zaps, a built-in [Cashu](/projects/cashu)
wallet for ecash payments, Nostr Wallet Connect for balance and payment flows,
and [NIP-46](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/46.md) remote
signing via Amber, nsec.app, Aegis, and Nowser. A built-in Tor client (via
[arti](https://gitlab.torproject.org/tpo/core/arti)) lets the app talk to
onion relays and Cashu mints directly, and a Discover tab exposes "NApps"
(third-party nostr web apps) through NIP-07.

## Why fund it?

Encrypted messaging is one of the few use cases where nostr has a chance to
replace an existing centralized product for everyday users. A usable mobile
client with group chats, calls, and an onboarding flow that people already
recognize from other messengers pushes that story forward. 0xchat is also one
of the earliest clients to ship new DM standards, which helps the rest of the
ecosystem converge on interoperable private messaging.

OpenSats first funded 0xchat in the
[second wave of nostr grants](/blog/nostr-grants-october-2023#0xchat) in
October 2023 and has renewed the grant multiple times, most recently in the
[thirteenth wave](/blog/thirteenth-wave-of-nostr-grants).

## What's next?

The project now ships two apps in parallel. The main 0xchat client keeps
adding features (Posts with Markdown, global feeds, translation via
LibreTranslate and Google ML Kit, NApps), and a smaller sibling called
[XChat](https://github.com/0xchat-app/xchat-app-main) (formerly "0xChat Lite")
focuses on native iOS and Android UIs and MLS-based "Circles" with their own
relays, storage, and file servers. Upcoming work covers disappearing
messages, Cashu wallet upgrades to support more NUTs,
[NIP-61 Nutzaps](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/61.md),
NIP-29 invite codes, and a BitChat integration that relays messages over
Bluetooth Low Energy mesh. The team ships regularly to the iOS App Store,
Google Play, [Zapstore](https://zapstore.dev/apps/com.oxchat.nostr), and
F-Droid.
