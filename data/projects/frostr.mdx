[Frostr](https://www.frostr.org/) is a threshold signing protocol for nostr
built on top of [FROST](https://eprint.iacr.org/2020/852) (Flexible
Round-Optimized Schnorr Threshold signatures). An `nsec` key is split into
shares that live on different devices or services. Any `t`-of-`n` quorum of
those shares can sign a nostr event together, but a single compromised share
does not leak the key. Shares can be rotated and replaced without rotating
the underlying identity, which matters on a protocol where your public key
_is_ your identity.

Coordination happens over nostr itself. Each share has its own nostr
identity and exchanges encrypted DMs with the other shares to produce a
signature, so there's no dedicated backend or custodian in the path.

The project ships as a stack of libraries and clients:

- [Bifrost](https://github.com/FROSTR-ORG/bifrost), the core FROST library
- [Igloo](https://github.com/FROSTR-ORG/igloo-desktop), a desktop app for
  managing shares
- [Frost2x](https://github.com/FROSTR-ORG/frost2x), a browser extension that
  signs nostr events with a share
- Heimdall for REST-API signing, Frostbite for mobile, and Permafrost for
  self-hosted signing nodes

## Why fund it?

The default key model on nostr puts one `nsec` on every device the user
touches. That works for casual accounts and fails for anyone with a
meaningful identity, financial tooling tied to their npub, or reason to
worry about device compromise. Threshold signing gives nostr a
production-grade option for multi-device keys, private recovery, and
institutional-style custody, all without changing how clients verify
signatures.

OpenSats funded Frostr in the
[twelfth wave of nostr grants](/blog/twelfth-wave-of-nostr-grants#frostr).
The work is also a building block for later protocols, for example bitcoin
coinjoins and Payjoins coordinated over nostr.

## What's next?

Near-term goals are a finished UX and a stable release for Igloo and
Frost2x, then filling out the rest of the stack: Heimdall for REST-API
signing, Frostbite for mobile, and Permafrost for self-hosted signing nodes.
The project also plans a
[NIP-46](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/46.md) bridge for
compatibility with existing remote-signing flows, a security audit, and
`sign_psbt` support for Bitcoin flows signed over nostr.
