Zapstore is a permissionless app store built on nostr. Developers sign and publish releases as events, users can verify binaries before installation, and discovery happens through an open social layer instead of a closed platform gatekeeper. The project supports Tor, local-first usage, and account-less flows, which makes it usable even in rough connectivity conditions.

Zapstore also includes tooling for developers. The `zapstore-cli` helps publish releases and manage app metadata, while the wider project has grown into a stack of relays, indexing infrastructure, signing tools, and app-store UX focused on open distribution.

## Why fund it?

Mobile and desktop software distribution is still dominated by a few centralized stores. That leaves developers exposed to arbitrary policy changes and leaves users with fewer ways to verify what they install. Zapstore pushes in the opposite direction: signed releases, open discovery, nostr-native identity, and bitcoin-native payments.

OpenSats supported Zapstore in the [tenth wave of nostr grants][wave-10]. The project helps strengthen the app distribution, discovery, and developer tooling that nostr needs to mature as an ecosystem.

## What's next?

Recent work includes the Zapstore 1.0 Android release, a redesigned package manager, custom app-catalog relay support, curated app stacks, background update checks, and major improvements to the website and indexing pipeline. The indexer now supports a much larger catalog, and the Android client has shipped steady 1.0.x releases.

Next steps focus on stabilizing the 1.0 app, improving relay and trust management, adding backup and restore for installed apps, expanding catalog-level trust and vouching, and making publishing even easier for developers.
