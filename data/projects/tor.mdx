The Tor Project builds and maintains one of the internet's most important privacy systems. Tor routes traffic through multiple relays so users can browse, communicate, and publish without handing the network a simple map of who they are and where they are connecting from.

This infrastructure matters well beyond web browsing. Tor underpins censorship resistance, protects vulnerable users, and provides critical network privacy for Bitcoin infrastructure. A large share of Bitcoin nodes use Tor onion services, which makes Tor's reliability and resilience directly relevant to Bitcoin's health.

## Why fund it?

Privacy infrastructure is easy to depend on and hard to fund. The Tor Project is a nonprofit, and work on onion service performance, reliability, and abuse resistance often lacks a clean commercial funding path even when the public benefit is obvious.

OpenSats supported Tor in December 2022 through a dedicated [support grant](/blog/tor-receives-support-grant) focused on onion services. The goal was straightforward: help Tor add developer capacity and improve a piece of privacy infrastructure that many Bitcoin users and node operators already rely on.

## What's next?

The work continues around onion service stability, denial-of-service resistance, metrics, and long-term maintainability. Improvements here strengthen Tor for everyone while also making Bitcoin's privacy-preserving network paths more dependable.

If you want the background on why this work matters, read the original [Tor support announcement](/blog/tor-receives-support-grant) and the privacy section of the [2024 year in review](/blog/2024-year-in-review).
