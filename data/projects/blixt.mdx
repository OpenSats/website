Blixt is a non-custodial Bitcoin Lightning wallet for Android, iOS, and macOS.
It runs [LND](https://github.com/lightningnetwork/lnd) and
[Neutrino](https://github.com/lightninglabs/neutrino) directly on the device,
so the wallet operates a full Lightning node without relying on centralized
servers for routing or chain data.

The wallet supports LNURL (pay, auth, withdraw, channel), keysend, multi-path
payments, a built-in WebLN browser, and Tor. Channel backups go to Google Drive
or iCloud. A feature called Speedloader helps the app start quickly and stay
in sync even on slower connections.

Hampus rebuilt the wallet's core around
[`react-native-turbo-lnd`](https://github.com/hsjoberg/react-native-turbo-lnd),
a C++ module that runs LND inside the app for faster payments without background
processes. He also improved Neutrino to connect to multiple peers in different
regions, making sync more reliable on mobile.

[Lightning Box](https://github.com/hsjoberg/lightning-box) is a trust-minimized
Lightning Address protocol built alongside Blixt. It lets users receive
payments at a human-readable address (user@domain) while keeping custody local.
A lightweight HTTP relay forwards requests to the wallet and returns an invoice.

## Why fund it?

Running a full Lightning node on a phone is the strongest form of self-custody
for Lightning payments. Blixt proves it works in practice and produces reusable
tools (`react-native-turbo-lnd`,
[`react-native-turbo-sqlite`](https://github.com/hsjoberg/react-native-turbo-sqlite),
Lightning Box) that other wallet projects can adopt.

OpenSats first funded Blixt in the [first wave of Bitcoin
grants](/blog/bitcoin-grants-july-2023#blixt-wallet) in July 2023 and has
[renewed the grant](/blog/renewing-our-commitment-to-bitcoin) since then.
Hampus also received a grant for [Noah](https://github.com/BlixtWallet/noah),
an Ark wallet, in the [sixteenth wave of Bitcoin
grants](/blog/sixteenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants).

## What's next?

Blixt 0.8.0 shipped with TurboLnd fully integrated. Hampus is now focused on
[Noah](https://github.com/BlixtWallet/noah), an [Ark](https://ark-protocol.org)
wallet built with
[`react-native-nitro-ark`](https://github.com/BlixtWallet/react-native-nitro-ark).
Ark solves some long-standing pain points for end-user wallets: offline
payments, easy onboarding without channels, and simpler recovery. Noah uses
Lightning under the hood to pay the outside world.

Desktop versions of Blixt are still in progress. A Linux build via Electron
has a working proof of concept. The Windows build is blocked by an upstream
issue in react-native-windows.

For a detailed look at earlier progress, see the [Advancements in Bitcoin and
Lightning Wallets](/blog/advancements-in-bitcoin-and-lightning-wallets#blixt-wallet)
impact report.
