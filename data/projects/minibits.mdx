Minibits is a mobile [Cashu](/projects/cashu) wallet built by
[Misovan](https://github.com/misovan). It runs on Android and iOS and lets
users hold ecash from multiple mints, pay over Lightning, and send tokens
directly to other users through Lightning addresses, nostr contacts, or plain
Cashu tokens.

The wallet integrates with Lightning in the usual ways (LNURL pay, LNURL
withdraw, Lightning addresses), and also supports nostr-native flows: you can
send ecash over [NIP-17](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/17.md)
encrypted DMs to any nostr contact. Tokens can be locked to a recipient's
public key, and a payment can be prepared while offline for the recipient to
claim later.

Minibits keeps the "bearer" property of ecash: tokens live on the device, not
on a server, and can be exported, imported, or transferred between phones
while keeping the same wallet address.

## Why fund it?

Cashu needs well-maintained wallets on the platforms people actually use. A
fast mobile client that supports the full stack of modern Cashu NUTs (payment
requests, WebSockets, cached responses, v4 token format, multi-mint balances)
tests the protocol under real-world load and surfaces bugs that only appear on
constrained devices.

OpenSats supported Minibits in the
[seventh wave of Bitcoin grants](/blog/bitcoin-grants-september-2024-7th-wave#minibits)
in September 2024 and renewed the grant in the
[sixteenth wave](/blog/sixteenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants). For a detailed
review of what the project shipped, see the
[Advancements in Ecash](/blog/advancements-in-ecash#minibits-wallet) impact
report.

## What's next?

An iOS build shipped via [TestFlight](https://testflight.apple.com/join/3QHbAfTg)
and via [Freedom Store](https://freedomstore.io/) for EU users. Recent work
added the compact v4 token format, animated QR codes for large tokens
([NUT-16](https://github.com/cashubtc/nuts/blob/main/16.md)), payment requests
([NUT-18](https://github.com/cashubtc/nuts/blob/main/18.md)), WebSocket
subscriptions for instant balance updates
([NUT-17](https://github.com/cashubtc/nuts/blob/main/17.md)), cached responses
for crash-safe payments ([NUT-19](https://github.com/cashubtc/nuts/blob/main/19.md)),
offline transfers, P2PK-locked tokens, and token export/import with
device-to-device migration.

Upcoming work focuses on more reliable [Nostr Wallet Connect](https://nwc.dev/)
flows, continued iOS polish, and keeping the wallet aligned with new Cashu
NUTs as they ship.
