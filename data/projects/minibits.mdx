Minibits is a mobile [Cashu](/projects/cashu) wallet built by
[Misovan](https://github.com/misovan). It runs on
[Android](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.minibits_wallet)
and [iOS](https://apps.apple.com/app/minibits/id6744454479) and lets users
hold ecash from multiple mints, pay over Lightning, and send tokens directly
to other users through Lightning addresses, nostr contacts, or plain Cashu
tokens. The app is available in English, Slovak, Spanish, and Portuguese.

The wallet integrates with Lightning in the usual ways (LNURL pay, LNURL
withdraw, Lightning addresses), and also supports nostr-native flows: you can
send ecash over [NIP-17](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/17.md)
encrypted DMs (sealed and wrapped via
[NIP-44](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/44.md)) to any
nostr contact. Tokens can be locked to a recipient's public key, and a payment
can be prepared while offline for the recipient to claim later. Every wallet
gets a persistent `name@minibits.cash` Lightning address routed through the
project's own server, which also handles zap notifications and Nostr Wallet
Connect delivery so payments reach the app even when it's backgrounded.

Minibits keeps the "bearer" property of ecash: tokens live on the device, not
on a server, and can be exported, imported, or transferred between phones
while keeping the same wallet address.

## Why fund it?

Cashu needs well-maintained wallets on the platforms people actually use. A
fast mobile client that supports the full stack of modern Cashu NUTs (payment
requests, WebSockets, cached responses, v4 token format, multi-mint balances)
tests the protocol under real-world load and surfaces bugs that only appear on
constrained devices.

OpenSats supported Minibits in the
[seventh wave of Bitcoin grants](/blog/bitcoin-grants-september-2024-7th-wave#minibits)
in September 2024 and renewed the grant in the
[sixteenth wave](/blog/sixteenth-wave-of-bitcoin-grants). For a detailed
review of what the project shipped, see the
[Advancements in Ecash](/blog/advancements-in-ecash#minibits-wallet) impact
report.

## What's next?

Android builds ship through
[Zapstore](https://zapstore.dev/apps/com.minibits_wallet). iOS is now
publicly available to EU users through
[Freedom Store](https://freedomstore.io/) (cooperation with the Vexl
foundation), with [TestFlight](https://testflight.apple.com/join/defJQgTD)
still serving the rest of the world. Recent work added the compact v4 token
format, animated QR codes for large tokens
([NUT-16](https://github.com/cashubtc/nuts/blob/main/16.md)), payment requests
([NUT-18](https://github.com/cashubtc/nuts/blob/main/18.md)), WebSocket
subscriptions for instant balance updates
([NUT-17](https://github.com/cashubtc/nuts/blob/main/17.md)), cached responses
for crash-safe payments ([NUT-19](https://github.com/cashubtc/nuts/blob/main/19.md)),
offline transfers, P2PK-locked tokens, token export/import with
device-to-device migration, and a challenge-response auth flow between the
wallet and the Minibits server inspired by
[NIP-42](https://github.com/nostr-protocol/nips/blob/master/42.md).

Upcoming work focuses on more reliable [Nostr Wallet Connect](https://nwc.dev/)
flows (especially under Android's background process limits), continued iOS
polish, hardening offline transactions based on real-world usage from places
like Cuba, and keeping the wallet aligned with new Cashu NUTs as they ship.
