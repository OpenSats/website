Soapbox started as an alternative Mastodon frontend, then built the [Mostr]
bridge to connect the fediverse to nostr. When OpenSats first funded the project
in 2023, [Ditto] was a nostr-native community server compatible with Mastodon
apps. Since then it has grown into a full organization building open-source
freedom tools across social media, AI-powered development, and activism, all on
nostr.

[Ditto] has evolved from that community server into a full social platform
where users customize their profiles, publish any kind of content, and stay
connected to followers on Bluesky and Mastodon through built-in bridges.
[Shakespeare] is an
open-source AI app builder that runs in the browser and works with any AI
provider. It was used to build Ditto 2.0 itself and has shipped as a standalone
product with its own Android app.

Alex also maintains [Nostrify], a nostr framework for [Deno] and the web, and
[MKStack], a full-stack toolkit for building nostr apps with AI.

## Why fund it?

Bridging nostr and the fediverse expands the reach of both networks. The [Mostr]
bridge alone exposes millions of fediverse users to nostr, and several have
already moved over entirely. Ditto bridges across content types within nostr
itself, bringing dozens of different micro experiences into a single platform.
Shakespeare extends that further by letting anyone build new experiences on
nostr and deploy them without touching a terminal.

OpenSats has been supporting Soapbox since the [first wave of nostr
grants][wave-1] in July 2023. Alex later received a [long-term support
grant][lts] in August 2024.

## What's next?

Soapbox is pushing the boundaries of nostr and making it the best and most
accessible place to build. Future plans include continuing to strengthen the
connection between Ditto and Shakespeare, improving the ability to create and
share social experiences, and improving the bridges between nostr and other
corners of the open web. The goal is to make nostr the fun and easy place for creators and communities
to build and thrive. People with no programming
experience are already using these tools to ship real applications, and that
feedback loop between building and using is only getting tighter.

In 2026, Soapbox is teaming up with the World Liberty Congress to launch
[Agora], a spin-off of Ditto built with Shakespeare tailored to activist
fundraising needs using Bitcoin. The team also plans to tackle Bitcoin
onboarding for general audiences, making sending sats to each other on nostr
easier than ever with no Bitcoin knowledge required.
