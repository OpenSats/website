The [OpenCash Association](https://opencash.dev/) is a Swiss nonprofit that
funds and coordinates open-source work on Chaumian ecash systems built on
Bitcoin. Its focus is the [Cashu](/projects/cashu) protocol, which lets
users hold and transfer sats-backed ecash tokens with strong privacy guarantees.
Mints issue tokens and handle Lightning settlement; users transact without
revealing their identity, balance, or counterparty to the mint.

OpenCash operates through grants, bounties, and community coordination rather
than shipping a single product. It funded a bounty for a
[BTCPay Server plugin](https://github.com/d4rp4t/BTCNutServer) that lets
merchants accept Cashu payments or auto-convert tokens to Lightning invoices. It
sponsored a Cashu track at the 2025 Bitcoin Designathon, producing wallet UX
patterns for setup, backup, multi-mint management, and NFC payments. It also
runs recurring public developer calls to keep implementations aligned on
standards like WebSocket subscriptions ([NUT-17](https://github.com/cashubtc/nuts/blob/main/17.md)) and payment requests ([NUT-18](https://github.com/cashubtc/nuts/blob/main/18.md)).

## Why fund it?

Cashu is a protocol, not a company. Many independent wallets, mints, and
libraries implement it, but someone has to fund the shared work: protocol
coordination, bounties for missing infrastructure, design resources, and
documentation. OpenCash fills that role.

OpenSats announced its support for OpenCash in
[Let a Thousand Flowers Bloom](/blog/let-a-thousand-flowers-bloom#opencash).

## What's next?

OpenCash maintains [opencash.dev](https://opencash.dev/) as the central hub for
grants and bounties, and contributes to [cashu.space](https://cashu.space/) and
[docs.cashu.space](https://docs.cashu.space/) as entry points for developers and
users. For a detailed look at recent progress, see the
[Advancements in Ecash](/blog/advancements-in-ecash#opencash) impact report.
