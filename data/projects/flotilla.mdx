Flotilla is a community-focused nostr client built by [Hodlbod][lts], the
creator of [Coracle](/projects/coracle). The model is "relays as groups": each
[relay](/topics/relays) is the home of a community, and the relay's own
configuration decides who can read and write there. A user joins a community by
adding the relay; a community owner sets policy by running, hosting, or paying
for the relay; and members get chat, threads, events, voice rooms, zaps, and
private messages without any of those features being routed through a central
operator.

The web app is a PWA. Users can join multiple communities at once, switch
between them like servers in Discord, and take part in synchronous chat or
asynchronous threads on the same relays. Other features include a calendar of
nostr events, [NIP-17](/topics/private-dms) gift-wrapped DMs, [web of
trust](/topics/web-of-trust) filtering, and "white-labeled" instances that an
operator can brand for their own community.

## Why fund it?

Most online communities live on platforms whose owners can change the rules,
demonetize the operator, or deplatform the members at any time. Self-hosted
alternatives exist, but each one is its own silo with its own accounts. Flotilla
puts community ownership back in the operator's hands by leaning on nostr
primitives that already work: a relay anyone can run, an identity that travels
between communities, and an event format that other clients can read. A user
who is part of ten Flotilla communities still has one keypair, one inbox, and
one social graph.

OpenSats supports Hodlbod through a [long-term support grant][lts], which is
what funds the ongoing work on Flotilla alongside Coracle and the
[Welshman](https://github.com/coracle-social/welshman) library that powers both
clients.

## What's next?

Coracle has moved into maintenance mode while Flotilla takes the lead. Active
work includes voice and video rooms, [Caravel](https://caravel.community/), a
relay-hosting platform built so non-technical operators can spin up a community
without running their own server, and [Pomade], an email-and-password login
protocol that lets newcomers join Flotilla without first learning about
keypairs.

For a detailed look at recent progress, see the [Advancements in Nostr
Clients][impact-report] impact report.
