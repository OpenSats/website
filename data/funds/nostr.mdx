The Nostr Fund is the pool of money OpenSats earmarks for nostr work. It pays
grants to relay operators, client developers, library maintainers, designers,
and protocol-level contributors who keep nostr running without a corporate
stack underneath it.

Past grants from this fund have gone to nostr clients on iOS, Android, web,
and desktop; to multiple relay implementations; to long-term support stipends
for senior protocol developers; and to design work that makes nostr usable for
people who are not already comfortable with the protocol. The list of related
announcements below shows who we have funded recently.

If you're working on nostr and your project needs funding to keep moving, you
can apply for a grant from this fund at [opensats.org/apply](/apply). The same
form covers nostr work and Bitcoin work; the board routes each application to
the relevant pool. You can see what we funded in the past by reading through our
[grant announcements](/tags/nostr). You can also see what these projects
shipped recently via OpenSats'
[Heartbeat](https://heartbeat.opensats.org/?funds=nostr).

Donate to the Nostr Fund if you want your money locked to nostr work. We
started this fund in 2023 with a contribution from Jack Dorsey's
[#startsmall](/blog/opensats-receives-additional-funding-of-dollar10m-from-startsmall)
and have been growing it from community donations since. Donations from US
taxpayers are tax deductible.
