Ville is an avid bitcoiner from Finland who learned the hard way. He writes a
blog at [kokkomaki.com](https://kokkomaki.com) haphazardly, yet commits to daily
vipassana meditation. His studies on nonviolence and peace culminated in a
master's thesis on Bitcoin and financial censorship. Since then, he has
independently explored Bitcoin through many lenses: feminism, social psychology,
human rights, culture, mercantilism, international relations, and various other
"isms." He advocates for financial sovereignty, digital privacy, and freedoms.

He is now building a dataset of financial censorship cases from around the world,
to be displayed on a website called [nofunds.org](https://nofunds.org). Ville
believes Bitcoin can help everyone, though proper education is absolutely
fundamental. He sees a dire need for open source software, especially as the
space grows more corporate and profit-seeking. That is why he is here. Ville
creates content alongside like-minded, enthusiastic people at OpenSats, and looks
forward to contributing, growing, and bringing everyone together.
